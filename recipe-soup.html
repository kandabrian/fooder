Tuscan White Bean & Rosemary Soup

This is the soup that Italians call ribollita's simpler cousin — humble in its ingredients, extraordinary in its comfort. Creamy cannellini beans, fragrant rosemary, a parmesan rind simmered into the broth, and enough good olive oil to make you feel like you're eating in Siena. Ready in 35 minutes, better than anything from a can.

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
25 min
Servings
4 people
Difficulty
Easy
Cuisine
Italian

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp good quality extra virgin olive oil, plus more to serve
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced thin
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 x 400g tins cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 1 litre good chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 parmesan rind (optional but highly recommended)
  • 100g cavolo nero or kale, thick stems removed, roughly chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Crusty bread to serve
  • Parmesan for grating over

Method

1
Build the base. Warm the olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the onion with a good pinch of salt and cook gently for 8–10 minutes until soft and translucent but not browned. Add the garlic and rosemary sprigs and cook for a further 2 minutes until fragrant.
2
Add the tomatoes. Pour in the chopped tomatoes and stir, scraping up any bits from the bottom of the pan. Cook for 5 minutes until the tomatoes have darkened slightly and the mixture smells rich and jammy.
3
Add beans and stock. Tip in the cannellini beans and stir to coat. Pour in the stock and add the parmesan rind if using. Bring to a gentle simmer, then cook uncovered for 15 minutes.
💡 The Parmesan Rind Trick

Never throw away your parmesan rinds. Store them in the freezer and drop one into any broth-based soup or stew. They melt slowly and add a deep, savoury umami that makes the whole dish taste like it simmered for hours.

4
Blend half the soup. Remove the rosemary sprigs and parmesan rind. Use a ladle to transfer about half the soup (beans and all) to a blender, or use an immersion blender directly in the pot. Blend until smooth then stir back into the chunky half. This gives the soup its signature creamy-yet-textured consistency.
5
Add the greens. Stir in the cavolo nero or kale and simmer for 5 minutes until wilted and tender. Season generously with salt and black pepper.
6
Serve. Ladle into bowls and finish with a generous drizzle of your best olive oil, a shower of freshly grated parmesan, and a few cracks of black pepper. Serve alongside thick slices of crusty bread — toasted and rubbed with a raw garlic clove if you're feeling indulgent.

Notes & Variations

This soup is fully vegetarian if you use vegetable stock and skip the parmesan rind (or use a vegetarian alternative). It's also excellent with a tin of good quality tuna flaked in at the end for a more substantial meal.

The soup thickens considerably as it sits — leftovers will need a splash of water or stock when reheating. It keeps well in the fridge for 4 days and freezes beautifully for up to 3 months.
