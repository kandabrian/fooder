Brown Butter Sage Gnocchi

Brown butter is one of the great transformations in cooking — ordinary butter, taken just a few degrees further, becomes something deeply nutty, caramel-sweet, and complex. Tossed with pillowy store-bought gnocchi and crispy sage leaves, it's a 25-minute dinner that tastes like you spent the afternoon in the kitchen.

Prep Time
5 min
Cook Time
20 min
Servings
2 people
Difficulty
Easy
Cuisine
Italian

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh or vacuum-packed potato gnocchi
  • 90g unsalted butter
  • 12–15 fresh sage leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, lightly crushed
  • 50g parmesan, finely grated, plus extra to serve
  • Zest of ½ a lemon
  • Flaky sea salt and black pepper
  • A small handful of toasted pine nuts (optional)

Method

1
Cook the gnocchi. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil. Cook the gnocchi according to packet instructions — usually 2–3 minutes — until they float to the surface. Reserve a cup of the starchy cooking water before draining.
2
Start the brown butter. While the gnocchi cooks, melt the butter in a large light-coloured frying pan over medium heat. A light pan is essential here — you need to see the colour change. Add the crushed garlic cloves.
3
Watch it carefully. The butter will foam, then subside. Keep swirling the pan. After about 4–5 minutes, you'll notice golden-brown specks forming at the bottom and a deeply nutty, biscuity aroma. This is the moment — it can go from perfect to burnt in seconds.
💡 How to Judge Brown Butter

The colour you're aiming for is the shade of a hazelnut. Once you see it, remove the pan from the heat immediately — the residual heat will continue cooking it. If it smells burnt or looks dark brown, start again. It's worth it.

4
Fry the sage. Once the butter is golden, remove the garlic and add the sage leaves. They'll sizzle and crisp up in 30–45 seconds. Remove them with a slotted spoon and set aside on paper towel.
5
Toss the gnocchi. Add the drained gnocchi to the brown butter pan over medium-low heat. Toss to coat, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water to create a light, glossy sauce. Cook for 1–2 minutes until the gnocchi picks up some golden colour.
6
Finish and serve. Remove from heat and stir in the parmesan and lemon zest. Season generously. Divide between warm bowls, top with the crispy sage leaves, pine nuts if using, and extra parmesan. Serve immediately.

Notes & Variations

This recipe works equally well with butternut squash gnocchi for an autumnal twist. You can also add wilted spinach or rocket stirred in at the end for some greenery. A small spoonful of ricotta on top adds a creamy contrast.

Brown butter doesn't reheat gracefully — this dish is best eaten immediately. Leftovers can be reheated in a pan with a splash of water, but the butter sauce won't fully come back.
