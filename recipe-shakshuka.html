Shakshuka with Feta & Fresh Herbs

Shakshuka is the breakfast that confidently doubles as dinner. Eggs poached directly in a richly spiced tomato and pepper sauce, finished with crumbled feta, fresh parsley, and a swirl of harissa. It's one pan, twenty minutes, and one of those dishes that makes everyone at the table feel like the day is going to be good.

Prep Time
5 min
Cook Time
15 min
Servings
2 people
Difficulty
Easy
Cuisine
Middle Eastern

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp sweet smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • Pinch of chilli flakes (or more, to taste)
  • 1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 4 large eggs
  • 80g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tbsp harissa paste (optional)
  • Fresh flat-leaf parsley and mint, to serve
  • Crusty bread or warm pitta, to serve

Method

1
Soften the vegetables. Heat the olive oil in a wide, deep frying pan or skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and red pepper with a pinch of salt. Cook for 7–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and beginning to sweeten. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute more.
2
Bloom the spices. Add the cumin, paprika, coriander, and chilli flakes to the pan. Stir constantly for 1 minute until the spices are fragrant and toasted — this step is what gives shakshuka its depth.
3
Add the tomatoes. Pour in the tinned tomatoes, add the sugar, and season with salt and pepper. Stir to combine and simmer over medium-low heat for 8–10 minutes until the sauce has thickened and the flavours have melded. Taste and adjust seasoning.
💡 The Egg Timing Secret

For runny yolks with set whites, cover the pan with a lid and cook for exactly 5–6 minutes. For fully set yolks, go 8–9 minutes. Every pan is different, so start checking at 5 minutes. Remove from heat slightly before they look done — they carry on cooking in the hot sauce.

4
Nestle in the eggs. Using the back of a spoon, make 4 wells in the tomato sauce. Crack an egg into each well. If using harissa, dot small spoonfuls around the pan between the eggs now.
5
Cover and cook. Place a lid on the pan and cook over medium-low heat for 5–8 minutes, depending on how you like your eggs. Keep the heat gentle — you want the eggs to steam, not bubble aggressively.
6
Finish and serve. Remove from heat and immediately scatter over the crumbled feta, fresh parsley, and mint. Bring the whole pan to the table and serve with good crusty bread or warm pitta for scooping up the sauce.

Notes & Variations

Shakshuka is wonderfully forgiving and easy to customise. Add a handful of baby spinach to the sauce before the eggs for extra greens. A tin of chickpeas stirred in makes it more substantial. For a Turkish-style version, swap the feta for beyaz peynir and add a dollop of cold yoghurt at the table.

The tomato sauce base can be made up to 3 days ahead and stored in the fridge. Just reheat, add the eggs, and you have breakfast on the table in under 10 minutes.
