About Saffron & Salt

A recipe blog for curious home cooks who believe that good food takes time — and occasionally, a pinch of saffron.

728 × 90 — Ad

Why This Blog Exists

Saffron & Salt started with a simple frustration: most recipe websites bury the actual recipe under paragraphs of personal anecdote, SEO keywords, and pop-up newsletters. We wanted something different — a clean, honest place where the recipe is front and centre and every dish has been tested until it's worth your time.

We cook the kind of food we want to eat. Weeknight dinners that are faster than a takeaway but taste like you tried. Weekend projects that reward patience. Soups that make the whole house smell like somewhere you'd want to be. And the occasional showstopper for when you want to feed people something they'll talk about on the drive home.

What We Believe About Cooking

⏳
Time is an Ingredient
Some of the best dishes take all day. A 7-hour lamb isn't difficult — it just needs time. We'll tell you when patience is the actual technique.
🧂
Season as You Go
Salt at every stage, not just at the end. Good seasoning is the difference between food that tastes flat and food that tastes alive.
🫒
Buy Less, Better
One bottle of excellent olive oil is worth more than five mediocre ones. We'd rather cook a simple dish with great ingredients than the reverse.
📖
Understand the Why
We explain why each step matters — not just what to do. A cook who understands technique improvises. A cook who only follows instructions gets stuck.

What to Expect

New recipes publish every Sunday. We cover a range of cuisines — Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, Italian, and beyond — united by a preference for bold flavour and unfussy presentation. We don't chase food trends. We chase dishes we'd genuinely want to eat on a cold Tuesday evening or a slow Sunday afternoon.

Every recipe on this site has been made, tasted, adjusted, and made again. If a dish didn't work in our kitchen, it doesn't make the site. If there's a shortcut worth knowing, we'll share it. If there's a step that sounds tedious but genuinely matters, we'll explain why.

A Note on Ingredients

Most of our recipes use widely available ingredients. When we call for something less common — like saffron, sumac, or good-quality preserved lemons — we'll always tell you why it matters and what to use instead if you can't find it. Cooking should be accessible, not exclusive.

We prefer unsalted butter (so we control the salt), good tinned tomatoes over fresh out of season, dried pasta for weeknights and fresh when it matters, and always — always — finishing with a drizzle of your best olive oil.
