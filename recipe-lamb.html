Slow-Roasted Lamb with Saffron Butter

There are recipes you make on a Tuesday and recipes you make for a reason. This is the latter. A whole leg of lamb, rubbed with saffron-infused butter, garlic, and rosemary, roasted low and slow until the meat surrenders completely from the bone. It's the kind of dish that fills a home with warmth long before it reaches the table.

Prep Time
20 min
Cook Time
7 hours
Servings
6 people
Difficulty
Easy
Cuisine
Mediterranean

Ingredients

  • 1 whole leg of lamb, about 2.5 kg (bone-in)
  • ½ tsp saffron threads
  • 2 tbsp warm water
  • 100g unsalted butter, softened
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves stripped and chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1½ tsp flaky sea salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 300ml chicken or lamb stock
  • 200ml dry white wine
  • 2 onions, roughly sliced
  • Fresh mint and flatbreads to serve

Method

1
Bloom the saffron. Combine the saffron threads with 2 tablespoons of warm water in a small bowl. Let it steep for 10 minutes — it should turn a deep golden orange. This step unlocks both the colour and the fragrance.
2
Make the saffron butter. Beat the softened butter with the saffron water, minced garlic, rosemary, cumin, cinnamon, lemon zest, salt, and pepper until everything is combined into a fragrant, golden paste.
3
Prepare the lamb. Using a small sharp knife, make 12–15 deep incisions all over the leg of lamb. Push a little of the saffron butter deep into each cut. Rub the remaining butter all over the surface. For best results, cover and refrigerate overnight. Bring to room temperature 1 hour before roasting.
4
Preheat and build the base. Preheat your oven to 160°C (fan 140°C / 325°F). Scatter the sliced onions across the base of a large roasting tin. Pour in the stock and white wine. Place the lamb on top of the onions.
5
Roast, covered, low and slow. Cover the tin tightly with two layers of foil. Roast for 6 hours. The meat will become incredibly tender and the juices will collect in the pan.
💡 Pro Tip

Don't rush the low-and-slow process. The collagen in the lamb needs time to break down into gelatine, which is what gives the meat its silky, pull-apart texture. A 7-hour lamb is infinitely better than a 4-hour one.

6
Blast and rest. Remove the foil and increase the oven to 220°C. Roast uncovered for a further 30 minutes until the surface is caramelised and golden. Remove from the oven and rest, loosely tented in foil, for 20 minutes.
7
Make the pan sauce. Pour the roasting juices through a sieve into a small saucepan. Skim off the fat and simmer over medium heat for 5–8 minutes until slightly reduced and glossy. Season to taste.
8
Serve. Pull the lamb apart with two forks directly in the tin. Serve with warm flatbreads, fresh mint, a dollop of yoghurt, and the pan sauce on the side.

Notes & Variations

If saffron is unavailable, substitute ½ tsp of turmeric and a pinch of smoked paprika for a similar golden colour, though the floral fragrance will differ. The overnight marination is optional but strongly recommended — it makes a noticeable difference to depth of flavour.

Leftovers keep well in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently with a splash of stock to keep the meat moist. Leftover lamb is exceptional stuffed into pitta with pickled onions and tahini.
